Explosion Protection Cheat Sheet

A quick-reference guide for engineers, technical sales, and product managers working with explosion-protected equipment. For full details on any topic, follow the links to the detailed files.

Quick Qualification Questions

When evaluating whether equipment is suitable for a hazardous area, answer these six questions in order:

# Question Where to Look
1 What zone is the area? (0, 1, 2 / 20, 21, 22) Zone Classification
2 Gas or dust? (or both?) Site hazard assessment
3 Required gas/dust group? (IIA/IIB/IIC or IIIA/IIIB/IIIC) Gas Groups
4 Required temperature class? (T1–T6 or specific °C for dust) Temperature Classes
5 Regional approval required? (ATEX / IECEx / NEC / other) Standards
6 Which protection method? (Ex d, Ex i, Ex e, Ex p, Ex n, etc.) Protection Methods

Zone → EPL

Zone EPL Required Protection Level

Hierarchy Rules

These "covers" rules simplify equipment selection:

  • Gas groups: IIC covers IIB and IIA — one IIC-rated device works everywhere
  • Dust groups: IIIC covers IIIB and IIIA
  • Temperature: T6 is the most restrictive and covers T5 through T1
  • EPL: Ga equipment can be used in Zones 0, 1, and 2

Strategic Considerations

Certification Determines Your Addressable Market

Certification Market Access
ATEX only EU/EEA + countries accepting ATEX
IECEx only ~35 countries worldwide (Australia, Singapore, Brazil, etc.)
ATEX + IECEx Most of the world except USA/Canada
ATEX + IECEx + UL/FM Global coverage

Design Impact of Protection Choice

Protection Design Complexity Cost Weight Maintenance
Ex ia High (circuit-level limits) Medium Low Low
Ex d High (precision machining) High High High
Ex e Medium (enhanced margins) Medium Medium Medium
Ex n Low (Zone 2 only) Low Low Low
Ex p Medium (pressure system) Medium-High Medium High (monitoring)

Certification Cost and Timeline

Typical ranges (vary significantly by product complexity and certification body):

Factor Range
Testing + certification fees €15,000 – €80,000+
Timeline (first certification) 6 – 18 months
Annual surveillance €3,000 – €10,000
Recertification (standard update) €5,000 – €30,000
Multi-scheme (ATEX + IECEx + UL) 1.5× – 2.5× single scheme

Product Strategy Decision Tree


Start
  ├── Zone 0 required?
  │     ├── YES → Ex ia or Ex ma (only options)
  │     └── NO ↓
  ├── Zone 1 required?
  │     ├── YES → Choose: Ex d (robust), Ex e (simpler), Ex ib, Ex p
  │     └── NO ↓
  ├── Zone 2 only?
  │     └── YES → Ex n (cheapest), Ex ec, Ex ic
  │
  ├── High power / switching?
  │     └── YES → Ex d (flameproof) or Ex p (pressurized)
  │
  ├── Low power / sensing?
  │     └── YES → Ex i (intrinsic safety)
  │
  └── Need to minimize weight/size?
        └── YES → Ex i or Ex m (encapsulation)

Common Certification Mistakes

  1. Starting certification too late — engage the Notified Body/ExCB early in design, not after prototyping
  2. Targeting too narrow — IIC T6 costs similar to IIA T1 but covers everything
  3. Ignoring regional requirements — ATEX doesn't cover Australia, IECEx doesn't cover USA
  4. No quality system — EN 13980 / ISO 80079-34 compliance needed before production
  5. Modifying after certification — any change to the certified design requires reassessment
  6. Underestimating documentation — technical files, test reports, instructions take months
